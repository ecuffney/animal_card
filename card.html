Aye-Aye

An Aye-Aye uses its long, creepy fingers to pull food out of holes in tree bark.

  • Scientific name: Daubentonia madagascariensis
  • Habitat: Madagascar
  • Diet: Omnivore
  • Average lifespan: 20 years

Although he may look like another lemur, the Aye-aye is a nocturnal primate endemic to the island of Madagascar. The Aye-aye spends its days curled in a ball of leaves high in the jungle canopy.
